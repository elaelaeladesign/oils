Nature’s Drops of Wellness

These small essential oil bottles are your travel-sized companions — only 2ml each, but still powerful in every drop. Because they’re so concentrated, remember: one tap on the bottom of the bottle equals about half a drop.

For internal use, add 1 drop to a large glass of water, or 2–3 drops per liter if you’re mixing a bottle. From these oils you can use Wild Orange, Peppermint, OnGuard and ZenGest for drinking.

For a simple aromatic boost, create your own 'aroma bowl': put 1–2 drops in your hands, rub them together gently, cup your hands over your face, and breathe in deeply. Hold for 10–15 seconds, then exhale slowly — feel the oils ground and uplift you. For example add 1 drop of Wild Orange and 1 drop of Peppermint for a refreshing, energizing aroma. For respiratory diseases use Air - more below.

Did you know that with direct topical application, about 50% of the oil evaporates into the surrounding air because essential oils are very volatile? When used with a carrier oil (fractionated coconut oil, almond oil, jojoba oil, etc.), about 80% of the oil penetrates the skin, even reaching deeper layers.

Wild Orange

Main Effects:

  • Energizing and uplifting: Promotes positivity, motivation, and balance.
  • Cleansing and purifying: Supports natural detox and purifies air and surfaces.
  • Immune support: Contains antioxidants (limonene) that strengthen the immune system.
  • Soothing and relaxing: Reduces stress while maintaining energy.

Common Uses:

  • Aromatically: Diffuse or inhale directly for a mood boost.
  • Topically: Apply diluted to wrists or temples for freshness and focus.
  • Internally: Add 1–2 drops to water or food for flavor and detox support.
  • Household: Mix with water to clean surfaces and freshen air.
  • Orange Butter: Add a few drops to soft butter for bread or use in the kitchen.
  • Mood lift / joy: Diffuse or inhale when feeling low; promotes positivity and lightness.
  • Creative boost: Place a drop on wrists or behind ears when doing creative work or brainstorming.

Caution:

  • Avoid sunlight or UV for 12h after topical use (photosensitive).
  • Always dilute before applying to skin.

ZenGest

Main Effects:

  • Supports healthy digestion and soothes occasional stomach upset.
  • Helps reduce feelings of bloating, gas, and indigestion.
  • Calms the body and promotes comfort after meals.

Blend Ingredients:

  • Peppermint
  • Ginger
  • Caraway
  • Coriander
  • Cardamom
  • Fennel
  • Star Anise

Common Uses:

  • Aromatically: Diffuse to calm the stomach and improve digestion.
  • Before and/or after meals: Drink a small glass of water with 1–2 drops of ZenGest and gently massage a few drops onto the stomach to support comfort and ease digestion.
  • For upset stomach: Rub a few drops directly on the stomach, preferably with carrier oil.
  • For travel nausea: Create an aroma bowl and inhale deeply to help settle your stomach.

Caution:

  • Use diluted for sensitive skin.
  • Keep away from eyes and mucous membranes.

Citronella

Main Effects:

  • Natural insect repellent.
  • Purifies and refreshes air.
  • Uplifts mood and promotes clear thinking.

Common Uses:

  • Aromatically: Diffuse outdoors to keep insects away.
  • Topically: Apply diluted before spending time outside.
  • Household: Add to cleaning solutions for freshness.
  • Insect / mosquito protection: Citronella is great for keeping mosquitoes and other flying insects away, and can be used alone or combined with Tea Tree and Lavender.
  • Energy clearing: Diffuse to clear stagnant energy in a room.
  • Protection: Can be used in combination with other protective oils like Tea Tree for a ritual space.

Caution:

  • Always dilute before topical use.
  • Avoid contact with eyes or sensitive areas.

Peppermint

Main Effects:

  • Boosts focus and energy.
  • Relieves tension and clears airways.
  • Cools and soothes the body.

Common Uses:

  • Aromatically: Inhale to wake up or feel alert.
  • Topically: Apply to temples, neck, or chest with carrier oil.
  • Internally: Add a drop to tea or water for digestive support.
  • Headaches: Apply with carrier oil to the temples or, in a darkened room, massage the big toes.
  • Refreshing / Heat / Concentration: Apply with carrier oil to temples, neck and behind ears.
  • Fresh Breath: Place a few drops directly on the tongue.
  • DIY Peeling: Mix a few drops with sugar/salt and coconut oil for an invigorating body scrub.
  • Focus & clarity: Inhale before studying or meditation to enhance concentration.
  • Cooling energy: Place a drop on temples during meditation to feel refreshed and alert.

Caution:

  • Keep away from eyes.
  • Use diluted on sensitive skin.

Lavender

Main Effects:

  • Promotes relaxation and calmness.
  • Supports restful sleep.
  • Soothes skin and minor irritations.

Common Uses:

  • Aromatically: Diffuse before bed or put on pillow for better sleep.
  • Topically: Apply to temples, neck, or irritated skin areas.
  • For cuts: Apply directly to the wound (without carrier oil).
  • For insect bites: Apply 1–2 drops directly to the bite to reduce itching and irritation.
  • Minor burns / sunburn: Apply a few drops to soothe the skin.
  • Headache / tension relief: Apply diluted on temples, neck, or behind the ears.
  • Skin irritation / rashes: Apply diluted to calm redness and inflammation.

Caution:

  • Use diluted for sensitive skin.

Tea Tree

Main Effects:

  • Purifies air and surfaces.
  • Supports skin health and clarity.
  • Promotes a clean, refreshed feeling.
  • Antibacterial, antiviral, and antifungal properties.

Common Uses:

  • Topically: Apply diluted to blemishes, pimples, or irritated skin.
  • Scalp care: Add a few drops to shampoo to soothe scalp irritation and reduce dandruff.
  • Insect / mosquito protection: In emergencies, apply a few drops to skin or clothing, or mix with water in a spray bottle for regular use. In my experience, Tea Tree works best for mosquito protection, but it can also be combined with Citronella and Lavender.
  • Cold sores: Apply a small amount of diluted Tea Tree oil directly to the affected area to help soothe and support healing (I gave the oils to you already by the time I got it :D)
  • Aromatically: Diffuse to cleanse and purify the air.
  • Before entering crowded places: Apply 1 drop to the crown chakra to help ward off negative energies.
  • Fungal issues: Can be applied (diluted) to nails or feet to support against fungal infections.

Caution:

  • For external use only.
  • Keep away from children and eyes.

OnGuard

Main Effects:

  • Supports immune system naturally.
  • Protects against environmental threats.
  • Promotes overall wellness and vitality.

Blend Ingredients:

  • Wild Orange
  • Clove
  • Cinnamon Bark
  • Eucalyptus
  • Rosemary

Common Uses:

  • Internally: Add a drop to water or recipes for immune support.
  • Topically: Dilute and apply to chest, neck, or bottoms of feet.
  • Household: Add to cleaning solutions to disinfect naturally.
  • For immune support / prevention: Rub onto the soles of the feet and chest.
  • Protective shield: Apply a drop to pulse points to support personal energetic boundaries.
  • Travel support: Keep a drop on a cotton pad in your bag or put on a scarf to inhale during flights or public transport for a refreshing scent and immune support.

A blend you might really enjoy — even in the kitchen. Try apple slices in water with one drop for a naturally spiced, cozy flavor — a touch of Christmas in a glass. Because of the clove content, this blend is also great for mouth, teeth, and throat. Use it as a gentle mouthwash or gargle when your throat feels sore.

Caution:

  • Use diluted for sensitive skin.
  • Do not ingest large amounts.

Air

Main Effects:

  • Clears and freshens the air.
  • Supports easy breathing and open airways.
  • Promotes calm and relaxation.

Blend Ingredients:

  • Laurel Leaf
  • Peppermint
  • Eucalyptus
  • Tea Tree
  • Lemon
  • Cardamom
  • Ravensara

Common Uses:

  • Aromatically: Diffuse to refresh the room and ease breathing.
  • Topically: Apply diluted to chest, neck and under/around the nose for soothing and relieving effect.
  • At the first sign of a cold / cough: Make an aroma bowl. Start with 1 drop to avoid too intense a smell. Another technique: put drops in one hand, form a tube with the other hand, and inhale directly.

Caution:

  • Use diluted for sensitive skin.
  • Keep out of reach of children and pets.
